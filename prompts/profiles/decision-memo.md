## Domain rubric: decision memo

The artifact under review is a decision document, not necessarily software; judge it as reasoning, not as a build plan. In addition to the general bar, each of the following is a material defect:

- An unstated assumption presented as established fact.
- Options compared on criteria that shift from one option to the next, or a rejected option never evaluated on the criteria the winner was.
- An irreversible commitment not flagged as irreversible.
- A claim of cost, effort, risk, or benefit asserted with no stated basis.
- A stakeholder who bears the consequences of the decision but never appears in the analysis.

Active probes — check these even where the brief and the memo are silent; the silence itself can be the objection:

- What would have to be true for the recommendation to be wrong, and does the memo say so?
- Which parts of the decision are one-way doors, and are they labeled as such?
- Is the strongest case against the recommendation stated in its strongest form, or a convenient weak form?
- Whose workload, budget, or risk does this decision move, and were they in the loop?

Severity calibration for this domain: unflagged irreversibility, assumption-as-fact, and inconsistent comparison criteria block approval; tone, structure, and formatting are non-blocking remarks at most.

These probes remain subject to the materiality bar above: raise a probe as an objection only when its answer would change the decision or its execution, not to demonstrate coverage.
